The problem

Native window management was not built for this.

macOS does a decent job when you are context-switching between full-screen apps. It does not help when you need one window — a terminal, an agent pane, a log viewer — to stay visible and in position while everything else moves around it. Mission Control, Stage Manager, and Split View all assume you want apps to share focus. You do not. You want one pane anchored, always.

The solution

Lintel enforces the layout you set.

Drag Lintel's divider once to split your screen into a left and right zone. Snap an app into either zone. Lintel watches that window and nudges it back into position if it drifts. No configuration files, no tiling profiles, no restart required. When you are done, drag the window past the divider and it floats free again.

A Claude Code session, a terminal, or any local AI agent pane stays anchored in its zone while you maximize, full-screen, or switch focus across your other windows.

Download for macOS

Requires macOS 13 or later. Apple Silicon and Intel supported.

Ad-hoc signed. On first launch, open System Settings → Privacy & Security and select Open Anyway.

Why does macOS warn me on first launch?

Lintel is not notarized through the Mac App Store. macOS Sequoia flags unnotarized apps on first open. This is expected. The app contains no background daemons and requests only Accessibility permission.

Lintel checks for updates automatically via Sparkle. You will be prompted before anything installs.
